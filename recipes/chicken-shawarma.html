Chicken Shawarma

Description

Nigerian chicken shawarma is a flavorful street-style wrap made with thinly sliced chicken marinated in spices like curry, thyme, garlic, ginger, and sometimes suya spice for extra heat. The chicken is grilled or pan-fried until smoky and juicy, giving it that bold, savory taste Nigerians love.

It's served in warm flatbread or pita with crunchy cabbage, carrots, onions, and tomatoes, then finished with a creamy, slightly spicy shawarma sauce. Toasted lightly after rolling, the shawarma comes out warm, spicy, and satisfyingâ€”perfect as a quick meal or snack.

Ingredients

Chicken Marinade

  • Chicken breast or thighs (boneless, thinly sliced)
  • Garlic
  • Paprika
  • Curry powder
  • Thyme
  • Black pepper
  • Suya spice
  • Salt
  • Vegetable oil

Shawarma Wrap

  • Flat or pita bread
  • Cabbage(thinly sliced)
  • Carrots(sliced)
  • Onions(sliced)
  • Tomatoes(sliced)

Sauce

  • Mayonnaise
  • Katchup
  • Chilli sauce or hot sauce(to taste)

Instructions

  1. Wash the chicken, pat dry, and cut into thin strips.
  2. In a bowl, combine garlic, ginger, paprika, curry powder, thyme, pepper, suya spice (if using), salt, and oil.
  3. Add the chicken to the marinade and mix well until fully coated. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Heat a pan or grill on medium-high heat and cook the chicken until well browned, slightly smoky, and fully cooked.
  5. While the chicken cooks, slice the cabbage, onions, tomatoes, and grate the carrots.
  6. Mix mayonnaise, ketchup, and chili sauce in a small bowl to make the shawarma sauce.
  7. Warm the flatbread or pita slightly to make it flexible.
  8. Place cooked chicken on the bread, add vegetables, and drizzle with the sauce.
  9. Roll the shawarma tightly, folding in the sides.
  10. Toast the wrapped shawarma lightly on a pan or grill for a crisp finish, then serve hot.
